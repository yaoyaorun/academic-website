Study Recruitment

Take Part in the Study

This page brings together the current recruitment routes for the Belonging Across Places study: an online survey, optional one-to-one interviews with migrant participants, and professional informant interviews at the intersection of cross-cultural support, technology, and AI.

Survey

15-20 minutes

Anonymous online survey for eligible East Asian migrant participants in the UK.

Interview

Optional one-to-one migrant interview about migration, belonging, support, digital tools, and AI.

Expert Interview

Around 60 min

Professional perspectives on cross-cultural support, migration, AI roles, boundaries, and future digital support.

Survey

Survey Invitation

English

Are you an East Asian migrant living in the UK? We are inviting first-generation East Asian migrants to take part in a Phase 1 PhD study at Imperial College London about relationships, belonging, help-seeking, and everyday use of digital tools or AI.

Eligibility

  • You are aged between 20-65.
  • You were born outside the UK, and the UK is currently one of your main places of residence.
  • You spent most of your childhood and teenage years in an East Asian cultural environment, such as mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan, Japan, South Korea, or an East Asian diaspora community elsewhere, such as in Southeast Asia.
  • You have lived in the UK for at least six months.
  • You currently hold a valid visa or legal status to reside in the UK, of any category including work, study, residency, or citizenship.

You will complete an anonymous online survey lasting about 15-20 minutes. The survey asks about migration experience, belonging, support needs, digital tools, and views on AI-supported services.

After submitting the survey, you may optionally enter a prize draw for 5 x £20 vouchers and/or leave contact details for future study contact. The Participant Information Sheet is provided at the start of the survey before consent. Participation is voluntary.

Survey link. Contact: Yao Xiao (yxiao3@ic.ac.uk).

Expert interview

Cross-cultural support, technology, and AI perspectives

We are looking for expert informants to take part in a research interview about how AI-supported tools might help people navigate cultural differences, support needs, and relationships across migration.

You may be eligible if you have relevant professional, research, or practice-based experience in areas such as cross-cultural support; migrant or community support; counselling or wellbeing; legal, immigration, housing, health, or student support policy; HCI/design; AI ethics, conversational AI, AI product practice, or entrepreneurship.

The interview will discuss your professional or practice-based observations. It may also include short fictional scenarios and AI response examples to explore appropriate AI roles, cultural attunement, boundaries, handoff, referral, and when AI should step back.

You do not need lived migration experience, direct experience supporting East Asian communities, or UK-based experience, provided your expertise is relevant to the study.

Format: online or in person. Duration: around 60 minutes, up to 90 minutes. Thank-you voucher: £30. No live AI system, prototype, or real user case will be involved.
